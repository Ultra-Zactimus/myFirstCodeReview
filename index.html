Why I Decided To Begin Learning At Epicodus

I have always had an interest in technology even at a young age. From the newest Televisions to Computers, Video Game Consoles, Telephones and so on. In the last 4-5 years I began to become interested in opening up game consoles and doing repair work and exploring programming; with the hopes of maybe someday making my own retro video game for a retro video game console like some independant developers. While I was never able to complete this undertaking it got my foot in the door for learning original C programming language.


In 2020 after getting a job at the Scotia Sawmill, I began to think a lot about my future and where I saw myself. I realized that I was no where near having the financial security I desired to be able to achieve some of the dreams I have. I wanted to buy my own home, I also wanted to be able to have the money to be able to travel and see the world. This realization that the area I live in had no good jobs to offer me a feasable means to these goals, then turned me on to looking into my options.


My little sister Julianna Green had attended and graduated from Epicodus several years prior. She went into this school with no knowledge of programming or what to expect other than a chance at a career and important skills that it would require. She now holds a job for a reputable company and has the financial freedom that she desired to build the future that she always wanted.


After consulting my sister about it, I finally decided to make the plunge. I spent almost 2 years working as much as I could so that I would be able to have the money to pay for my tuition and be able to be unemployed for 6 months so that I could focus my entire attention on learning coding and the skills needed to solve problems at Epicodus. I am incredibly thankful and excited to have this experience.

Later down the road when I started my last job, my General Manager and I started to play games after work was over.
